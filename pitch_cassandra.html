The Problem

Partners are the most underprepared people
in every birth room in America.

Every year, 3.6 million babies are born in the United States. In almost every one of those births, there is a partner standing in that room — often frightened, often silent, often feeling completely useless — because no one ever taught them what to do.

Traditional childbirth education focuses almost entirely on the person giving birth. The partner is an afterthought. The result is documented in peer-reviewed research:

80%
of first-time partners report childbirth-related fears — the #1 driver is lack of knowledge
O'Brien, Doherty & McNamara · IJERPH · 2021
60.2%
of episiotomies performed without informed consent being sought
Jacques et al. · Int'l Journal of Gynecology & Obstetrics · 2025
39%
reduction in cesarean risk when a prepared support person is continuously present
Bohren et al. · Cochrane Review · 2017 · 16,000 women

The data is unambiguous: a prepared, continuously present partner produces measurably better birth outcomes. Fewer interventions. Shorter labors. Higher satisfaction. Lower postpartum depression rates for both parents. The preparation doesn't exist at scale. That's the gap Pregnancy Partnership fills.
